Problem Statement

Traditional evaluation methods rely on a final exam or assessment to measure a student's growth. However, exam scores often give noisy, inaccurate results, especially for students who aren't good test-takers.

In our project, we explore the ways a studentâ€™s process can be used as a better measurement of their growth in ability. To examine these effects, we will use student submissions in the Hour of Code task on Code.org, and try to evaluate students using modern ML techniques.

Dataset

10 problems, 45,000 students, 100,000 submissions.

First Submission

This student does not have much code yet, but likely has alot of room to grow. Submissions like these can indicate that the student will learn a lot from this exercise.

Last Submission

The student has much more code now. They have a nested loop structure and move/turn statements. The student has made a lot of progress since their first submission, which is a very good sign!

Rubric Scoring

These are examples of rubric items we assign to each individual submission. In our project, we also explore how a student's rubric scores change over time.
